Bussiness Books

Atomic habits

A deep dive into the power of small habits and how they can lead to significant personal and professional growth. This book offers actionable strategies for building better habits and breaking bad ones, making it a must-read for anyone looking to improve their business acumen and productivity.

The Lean Startup

Ries introduces the concept of a lean startup, emphasizing the need for startups to be more efficient and responsive. The book advocates for building a minimum viable product (MVP), testing assumptions, and pivoting based on customer feedback

Good To Great

This book explores how companies transition from being good to great and identifies the key characteristics that enable them to sustain that greatness. Collins and his research team analyzed a set of companies over a period of time to uncover what distinguishes great companies from mediocre ones.

Blue Ocean Strategy

The authors present the idea of "blue ocean" strategy, where companies can succeed by creating new market space (a blue ocean) rather than competing in overcrowded existing industries (red oceans). The book offers frameworks and tools for creating such strategies.

Thinking Fast and Slow

Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman takes readers on a groundbreaking tour of the mind, explaining the two systems that drive our thoughts. System 1 is fast, intuitive, and emotional; System 2 is slower, more deliberative, and logical. Kahneman explores how these systems shape our judgments and decisions.

Zero To One

Peter Thiel, co-founder of PayPal, shares his insights on building startups that create new things. Thiel argues that true innovation comes from creating something unique (going from zero to one) rather than copying existing ideas.
